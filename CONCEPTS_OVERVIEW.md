## The Problem It Solves

AI coding assistants are brilliant and forgetful. They build fast, but every session starts from zero. For solo founders that creates two silent killers:

1. **Drift** — the assistant proposes elegant solutions that don't match where your project actually is, and quietly over-engineers.
2. **Re-discovery** — every session re-learns the same painful lessons; nothing accumulates.

CoveLab Foundation is a discipline layer that fixes both. It turns a forgetful-but-fast assistant into a system that stays on plan and gets *less* error-prone over time — the opposite of what usually happens.

It was built under its own discipline for 45 days of daily real-world use before it was ever offered for sale. What you get has substance behind it, not a weekend's worth of folders.

## The Big Advantage: It Works On Its Own — While You Stay In Control

The point isn't just "AI that helps." It's AI that **runs tasks on its own without wandering off the plan** — and hands the real decisions back to you.

- **The machine executes. You decide.** It works through tasks autonomously and pauses at the checkpoints *you* set. You approve direction, scope, and release; it does the legwork.
- **Autonomous, but not drifting.** As it works on its own, deviations from the plan are caught early — not discovered at launch. Most tools only keep this discipline while you're chatting live; here it holds up when tasks run unattended too.
- **Proven, honestly.** In our own 4-day live test it completed 64 tasks back-to-back with a 92.2% success rate and zero crashes. That's a self-measured result on our own project — not a lab benchmark and not a claim against any other product. We show it because it's real.

This combination — runs by itself, stays on plan, you keep the final say — is the heart of CoveLab Foundation.

## What's Inside (at a glance)

CoveLab Foundation is an **operations stack**, not a template. A template is inert and you copy it; an operations stack actually runs and keeps your project on track. These are the pillars — the *purpose* of each, without the inner workings:

- **A 6-layer structure** — everything has one clear home, from the generic framework at the bottom to your specific project at the top. You only ever customize the top layer; the rest stays stable.

- **The Lock-Mechanic** — your project declares one objective as "locked," and distractions (side-projects, premature marketing) are blocked until it's done. It externalizes the discipline so you don't rely on willpower. Only you can release the lock, and only when the goal is genuinely met.

- **Phase-Gates** — work is grouped into phases, and you can't jump ahead until the current phase is truly finished. It feels restrictive; in practice it's freedom from half-finished pile-ups.

- **Two-layer anti-drift** — the discipline that makes the autonomy above safe. It keeps the plan on track both when you're chatting live *and* when tasks run on their own in the background. The background case is where most comparable tools fall short (they only cover the live case) — it's what lets tasks run unattended without drifting.

- **A state-machine for progress** — every step of progress is recorded cleanly, so you never end up with "half-done" work that looks finished but isn't.

- **~30 built-in rules** — each one exists because a specific mistake was made once and never again. You don't memorize them; the system applies them for you.

- **A 3-role way of working** — a clear split between *deciding*, *planning*, and *building*, so mistakes get caught early and nobody becomes a bottleneck.

- **A growing pitfall database** — 70+ documented stumbling blocks with the lesson behind each one. This is the part that makes the system compound: your project six months in is meaningfully less error-prone than on day one — automatically.

- **One source of truth** — the whole system runs in one place, so nothing drifts out of sync. You can even steer it from your phone.
